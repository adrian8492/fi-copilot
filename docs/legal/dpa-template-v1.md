# Data Processing Addendum (DPA) — v1

**Effective:** Upon execution
**Customer (Dealership):** _______________________
**Vendor:** ASURA Group, Inc. ("ASURA")
**Product:** F&I Co-Pilot

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Master Services Agreement (or equivalent) between Customer and ASURA. Where this DPA conflicts with that agreement, this DPA governs with respect to data protection.

---

## 1. Definitions

- **Customer Data:** any information about the Customer's customers ("End Customers") submitted to or generated by F&I Co-Pilot, including PII, deal data, audio recordings, transcripts, and derived analytics.
- **NPI (Non-Public Information):** as defined under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and FTC Safeguards Rule.
- **Sub-processor:** a third party engaged by ASURA that processes Customer Data on ASURA's behalf.
- **Personal Data:** any information relating to an identifiable End Customer.
- **Process / Processing:** any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, transmission, transformation, deletion.

## 2. Scope of Processing

ASURA processes Customer Data solely to:

1. Provide the F&I Co-Pilot service to Customer.
2. Generate transcripts, coaching insights, compliance flags, and analytics for Customer's internal use.
3. Improve product quality (in aggregate, de-identified form).
4. Comply with legal obligations.

ASURA will not process Customer Data for any other purpose without Customer's prior written consent.

## 3. Customer Responsibilities

Customer represents and warrants that:

1. It has lawful basis (consent, contract, or legitimate interest under applicable law) to share End Customer data with ASURA.
2. It has obtained consent from End Customers as required by applicable wiretap and recording-consent laws (including but not limited to RCW 9.73.030 in Washington and Penal Code §632 in California).
3. It will use the in-app two-party consent flow for live recording, or provide equivalent customer notice and consent through other documented means.
4. It will not upload data of End Customers under the age of 13.

## 4. Security Measures

ASURA implements and maintains:

- **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.2 or higher for all customer data flows.
- **Encryption at rest:** AES-256-GCM for sensitive PII columns.
- **Multi-tenant isolation:** every database query is scoped to the Customer's dealership identifier; cross-tenant access is impossible at the query layer.
- **Access controls:** role-based access (admin, group-admin, super-admin) with audit logging on every access.
- **Audit logging:** every access to End Customer data is logged with userId, action, IP, and timestamp; logs are retained for seven (7) years.
- **Vulnerability management:** annual third-party penetration testing.
- **Incident response:** documented breach-notification procedures (see Section 7).

## 5. Sub-processors

ASURA uses the following sub-processors to provide F&I Co-Pilot. Each is bound by a written agreement with terms no less protective than this DPA.

| Sub-processor | Service | Data Touched | Region |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deepgram, Inc. | Live audio transcription | Audio + transcripts | US |
| Resend, Inc. | Transactional email | Email content + recipient address | US |
| ASURA-managed MySQL | Application database | All Customer Data | US (Pacific Northwest) |
| Cloudflare, Inc. | DNS + tunneling | Request metadata only | US |
| GitHub, Inc. | Source-code hosting | Code only — no Customer Data | US |

ASURA will provide thirty (30) days' notice before adding or replacing a sub-processor that materially changes the scope of data shared. Customer may object to any new sub-processor in writing within fifteen (15) days of notice; if the parties cannot agree on a remedy, Customer may terminate the affected portion of the service.

## 6. End Customer Rights

ASURA will support Customer in honoring End Customer requests for:

- **Access:** export of the End Customer's data set within 5 business days of request.
- **Correction:** updating fields that the Customer is permitted to update.
- **Deletion:** soft-delete within 24 hours of request; hard-delete on day 31 unless the request is cancelled. Cancellation is allowed during the 30-day window.
- **Restriction of processing** during dispute investigation.

These requests are submitted through F&I Co-Pilot's built-in **Data Deletion Request** flow or by email to `compliance@asuragroup.com`.

## 7. Breach Notification

If ASURA becomes aware of a Security Incident affecting Customer Data, ASURA will:

1. Notify the Customer's designated contact within seventy-two (72) hours.
2. Provide reasonable detail on the nature, scope, and likely impact of the Incident.
3. Document the Incident, the response, and the remediation in a written summary.
4. Cooperate with Customer's response, including providing necessary information to satisfy state breach-notification laws.

A "Security Incident" means a confirmed unauthorized access to, or disclosure of, Customer Data — not a routine probe, attempted intrusion, or operational disruption.

## 8. Data Retention

- **Audio recordings:** 90 days unless retained by Customer for a documented business purpose.
- **Transcripts and coaching artifacts:** retained for the term of the agreement plus 90 days.
- **Deal records:** retained indefinitely (financial-record retention requirement).
- **Audit logs:** retained for seven (7) years.

End Customer-initiated deletion requests override these defaults.

## 9. Term and Termination

This DPA remains in effect for the term of the underlying agreement. On termination:

1. ASURA will return or delete all Customer Data within thirty (30) days of Customer's written request.
2. ASURA may retain data required by law (e.g., audit logs, financial records) for the duration mandated by law.
3. Customer may export its data via F&I Co-Pilot's export functionality at any time during the term and for thirty (30) days after termination.

## 10. Compliance Standards

ASURA commits to maintaining compliance with:

- **FTC Safeguards Rule** (16 CFR Part 314, 2023 update).
- **State breach notification laws** in all US jurisdictions where End Customers reside.
- **Washington recording-consent law** (RCW 9.73.030) — two-party consent enforced for all dealerships regardless of state.
- **TILA / Regulation Z** disclosures supported in the deal-review and grading flows.
- **ECOA / Regulation B** — F&I Co-Pilot does not make automated lending decisions; lender submissions are routed by the F&I manager.

ASURA pursues SOC 2 Type 1 attestation, expected by Q3 2026.

## 11. Audits

Customer may, on reasonable notice and not more than once per year, request an audit of ASURA's controls relevant to Customer Data. ASURA will respond with a current SOC 2 report (when available), penetration-testing summary, and access to its compliance posture documentation. On-site audits require advance written agreement.

## 12. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the State of Washington, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute will be resolved in the state or federal courts located in King County, Washington.
