# F&I Co-Pilot Grading Rubric & Implementation Guide

## 1. Overview

### Purpose of the Rubric
This rubric serves as the definitive evaluation standard for F&I manager performance within the Co-Pilot system. It measures strict adherence to the established 23-script framework and 10-product menu presentation sequence. The system is designed to ensure consistent, compliant, and effective customer interactions while maintaining legal and regulatory standards.

### Co-Pilot System Integration
The grading rubric integrates with the Co-Pilot system through:
- **Real-time monitoring**: Live session scoring during customer interactions
- **Post-session analysis**: Comprehensive evaluation of recorded sessions
- **Performance tracking**: Historical data compilation for individual and team metrics
- **Coaching recommendations**: Automated feedback generation based on deviation patterns
- **Compliance reporting**: Regulatory adherence documentation

### Scoring Philosophy: Strict Adherence to Verbatim Scripts
The evaluation system operates on the principle that the provided scripts represent the optimal, legally compliant, and most effective approach to F&I interactions. Any deviation from these scripts is measured and penalized according to the severity of the variance. The system recognizes that while natural conversation flow is important, the core messaging, sequence, and required phrases must remain intact to ensure:
- Legal compliance and regulatory adherence
- Consistent customer experience across all interactions
- Optimal product presentation and closing effectiveness
- Proper needs discovery and objection handling

## 2. Process Stage Rubric (23 Scripts Mapped to 7 Stages)

### Stage 1: Introduction (Scripts 1-2) — Weight: 10%

**Included Scripts:**
- Script 1: Professional Hello
- Script 2: Retail Delivery Preparation Worksheet (Client Survey) - Introduction portion

**Required Verbatim Phrases:**
- "Congratulations on your new vehicle! My name is _________, and I am one of the business managers here at ____________ dealership."
- "I have a few responsibilities today: I am going to complete all your state and federal documents; then I will review your manufacturer's warranty; and last and most importantly, I am going to get you out of here as quickly as possible for you to start enjoying your new vehicle."
- "Now, we have developed a quick client survey to speed up the rest of this process, which I am going to get into now."

**Sequence Requirements:**
1. Congratulations and introduction MUST come first
2. Responsibilities outline MUST follow introduction
3. Client survey transition MUST be the final element

**Scoring Criteria (0-100 scale):**
- **Verbatim Match (90-100)**: All required phrases delivered exactly as written with minor acceptable variations (um, uh, natural speech patterns)
- **Acceptable Paraphrase (70-89)**: Core concepts present with slight word variations but all key elements maintained
- **Significant Deviation (40-69)**: Some required elements present but phrasing materially different or elements out of sequence
- **Critical Miss (0-39)**: Required phrases completely absent, contradictory information provided, or major sequence violations

## 4. Script Fidelity Scoring Methodology

### Verbatim Match Percentage Calculation

The system employs a dual-analysis approach combining deterministic keyword matching with semantic evaluation:

**Deterministic Engine Process:**
1. **Text Preprocessing**: Remove filler words (um, uh, like) while preserving core content
2. **Key Phrase Extraction**: Identify all required verbatim phrases from the script library
3. **Keyword Overlap Analysis**: Calculate percentage match using formula:
   ```
   Match Percentage = (Matched Keywords / Total Required Keywords) × 100
   ```
4. **Sequence Verification**: Ensure required phrases appear in prescribed order
5. **Completeness Check**: Verify all mandatory elements are present

**LLM Evaluator Process:**
1. **Semantic Analysis**: Assess whether paraphrased content maintains original meaning
2. **Intent Preservation**: Evaluate if customer experience objectives are met
3. **Compliance Review**: Identify any regulatory or legal requirement violations
4. **Context Evaluation**: Determine if variations enhance or detract from script effectiveness

### Key Phrase Extraction Method

**Primary Phrase Categories:**
1. **Mandatory Openers**: Required introductory statements
2. **Core Benefit Statements**: Essential value propositions
3. **Compliance Disclosures**: Legally required information
4. **Transition Phrases**: Required connectors between sections
5. **Closing Elements**: Essential assumption and completion statements

**Extraction Algorithm:**
- Identify phrases marked with quotation marks in scripts as mandatory verbatim
- Classify phrases by importance level (Critical, Important, Preferred)
- Weight phrases based on legal/compliance requirements vs. sales effectiveness
- Create phrase dependency maps showing required sequence relationships

### Acceptable vs. Unacceptable Deviations

**Acceptable Deviations:**
- Natural speech patterns and filler words
- Customer name personalization
- Specific vehicle/price information insertion
- Minor word order changes that preserve meaning
- Appropriate emotional inflection and emphasis

**Unacceptable Deviations:**
- Omission of compliance-required phrases
- Addition of claims not supported by script
- Sequence violations that impact customer understanding
- Word changes that alter legal meaning or coverage scope
- Failure to pause for required customer acknowledgments

### Scoring Integration

**Final Score Calculation:**
```
Script Fidelity Score = (Deterministic Match × 0.6) + (LLM Semantic Score × 0.4)
```

This weighting ensures that exact phrase matching takes precedence while allowing for evaluation of natural conversation flow and customer rapport building.

## 5. Deviation Taxonomy

### VERBATIM_MATCH (Score: 90-100)
**Definition**: Exact or near-exact delivery of required phrases with only minor acceptable variations.

**Characteristics:**
- All mandatory phrases delivered exactly as written
- Natural speech patterns and filler words acceptable
- Proper sequence maintained throughout
- Customer-specific information (names, amounts) correctly inserted
- Required pauses and acknowledgments obtained

**Examples:**
✅ Script: "Congratulations on your new vehicle!"
Delivered: "Congratulations on your new vehicle, Mr. Smith!"

✅ Script: "I have a few responsibilities today"
Delivered: "I have, um, a few responsibilities today"

### ACCEPTABLE_PARAPHRASE (Score: 70-89)
**Definition**: Core meaning and intent preserved with different wording, no critical information lost.

**Characteristics:**
- Essential concepts communicated effectively
- Minor word substitutions that maintain meaning
- Sequence generally maintained with minor variations
- Customer objectives still achieved
- No compliance violations introduced

**Examples:**
✅ Script: "I am going to get you out of here as quickly as possible"
Delivered: "I'm going to get you through this process as fast as possible"

✅ Script: "What is your understanding of the factory warranty?"
Delivered: "What do you know about your factory warranty coverage?"

### SIGNIFICANT_DEVIATION (Score: 40-69)
**Definition**: Key concepts present but phrasing materially different, some effectiveness lost.

**Characteristics:**
- Core message recognizable but poorly delivered
- Important phrases omitted or significantly altered
- Sequence violations that impact customer understanding
- Some required elements missing
- Customer experience potentially compromised

**Examples:**
❌ Script: "This is not an extended warranty... only comes into play when a part is deemed defective"
Delivered: "This is different from your warranty because it covers more things"

❌ Script: Complete 20-question survey
Delivered: Only 12 questions asked, several out of sequence

### CRITICAL_MISS (Score: 0-39)
**Definition**: Required elements completely absent, contradicted, or delivered in ways that compromise compliance or effectiveness.

**Characteristics:**
- Mandatory phrases completely omitted
- Contradictory information provided
- Major sequence violations
- Compliance requirements ignored
- Customer confusion or misinformation likely

**Examples:**
❌ Script: "Vehicle service agreement is not an extended warranty"
Delivered: "This is basically an extended warranty"

❌ Script: All 10 products in specific sequence
Delivered: Only 5 products presented, random order

### SEQUENCE_ERROR (Score: Deduction Applied)
**Definition**: Correct content delivered in wrong order, impacting customer understanding or legal compliance.

**Scoring Impact:**
- Minor sequence violations: -5 to -15 points
- Major sequence violations: -15 to -30 points
- Critical sequence violations (compliance-related): -30 to -50 points

**Examples:**
❌ GAP presentation before VSC (violates dependency logic)
❌ Closing attempted before objection handling
❌ Compliance disclosures delivered out of required order

### UNAUTHORIZED_ADDITION (Score: Deduction Applied)
**Definition**: Manager added claims, promises, or information not contained in approved scripts.

**Risk Categories:**
- **High Risk** (-50 points): Legal/compliance violations, unsupported product claims
- **Medium Risk** (-25 points): Off-script promises, unauthorized pricing discussions
- **Low Risk** (-10 points): Excessive personal anecdotes, irrelevant information

**Examples:**
❌ "This warranty covers everything, no exceptions" (overpromising)
❌ "I can get you a better rate if you buy these products" (illegal tying)
❌ Lengthy personal stories unrelated to the script content

## 6. Implementation Guide for Co-Pilot Demo

### Grader System Usage for Recorded Sessions

**Pre-Evaluation Setup:**
1. **Session Import**: Upload audio/video file to Co-Pilot platform
2. **Transcription Review**: Verify accuracy of automated transcription
3. **Customer Information Mapping**: Ensure correct vehicle, pricing, and customer data
4. **Script Template Selection**: Choose appropriate script set based on customer type

**Evaluation Process:**
1. **Stage-by-Stage Analysis**: Evaluate each of the 7 process stages independently
2. **Product Presentation Review**: Score each of the 10 products individually
3. **Deviation Classification**: Mark each variation using the 6-category taxonomy
4. **Sequence Verification**: Ensure proper order maintained throughout presentation
5. **Completeness Audit**: Verify all required elements covered

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Timestamp each script delivery for sequence verification
- Note exact phrases delivered vs. required phrases
- Record customer responses and manager reactions
- Flag any unauthorized additions or critical omissions
- Calculate individual stage scores and overall weighted score

### Live Session Real-Time Scoring

**Real-Time Monitoring:**
- **Audio Processing**: Continuous speech-to-text conversion with 2-3 second delay
- **Script Matching**: Immediate comparison against required phrase library
- **Stage Tracking**: Automatic progression tracking through the 7-stage process
- **Alert System**: Real-time notifications for critical deviations or omissions

**Manager Dashboard Elements:**
- Current stage progress indicator
- Next required phrase display
- Deviation alert notifications
- Customer response prompts
- Completion percentage tracker

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Running score calculation updated every 30 seconds
- Immediate feedback on phrase matching accuracy
- Stage completion confirmations
- Final score compilation upon session completion

**Successful Match Criteria:**
- Verbatim delivery within 90-100% accuracy range

**Deviation Triggers:**
